11th House

Friendships • Community • Groups • Hopes • Dreams • Vision

The 11th House governs friendships, groups, and collective vision. It shows your relationship with community, your hopes and dreams for the future, and how you contribute to collective progress. This is the house of friendship, innovation, and social consciousness.

Core Themes

  • Friendships: Platonic relationships, chosen family, allies
  • Groups: Organizations, clubs, social movements, networks
  • Hopes & Dreams: Future vision, aspirations, ideals
  • Community: Collective belonging, social causes
  • Innovation: Progress, technology, humanitarian efforts
  • Detachment: Objective perspective, intellectual connection

Natural Ruler

Aquarius & Uranus: The 11th house is naturally associated with Aquarius (fixed air) and Uranus (revolution, innovation). This gives the house themes of progressive ideals, collective consciousness, and friendship-based connection.

Planets in the 11th House

Sun: Identity through friendships and groups. Natural networker and visionary.

Moon: Emotional security through community. Nurtures friends and groups.

Mercury: Communicates with groups, intellectual friendships. Networks effectively.

Venus: Values friendships highly, harmonious groups. Attracts friends easily.

Mars: Activist energy, fights for causes. Can be combative in groups.

Jupiter: Abundant friendships, optimistic vision. Expands through community.

Saturn: Serious friendships, disciplined groups. May feel isolated or have few but loyal friends.

Uranus: Natural placement—unconventional friendships, revolutionary vision. Sudden changes in social circles.

Neptune: Idealized friendships, spiritual community. Can be delusional about groups.

Pluto: Intense friendships, transformative groups. Power dynamics in community.

Jungian Interpretation

Psychological Theme: The 11th house represents the collective unconscious, the integration of individuality with community, and the vision of what could be. It's where we connect to something larger than ourselves while maintaining our uniqueness.

Shadow: Emotional detachment, losing self in group identity, unrealistic idealism, alienation

Integration: Balancing individuality with belonging, vision with practicality, detachment with connection

Shadow Work Questions

  • Do I lose myself in group identity?
  • Am I using detachment to avoid emotional intimacy?
  • Are my hopes and dreams realistic or escapist?
  • How can I serve the collective without abandoning myself?
  • Where do I feel alienated from community?
