7th House

Partnerships • Marriage • Contracts • Descendant • The Other

The 7th House governs committed partnerships, marriage, and all one-on-one relationships. It shows what you seek in a partner, how you relate, and what you project onto others. The Descendant marks the cusp of this house—the setting point, representing the qualities you seek in "the other."

Core Themes

  • Marriage: Committed partnerships, spouse, long-term relationships
  • Contracts: Legal agreements, business partnerships
  • Projection: Qualities you disown and seek in others
  • Open Enemies: Known opponents, conflicts
  • Balance: Learning to relate, compromise, cooperation
  • The Other: What completes you, your opposite

Natural Ruler

Libra & Venus: The 7th house is naturally associated with Libra (cardinal air) and Venus (love, harmony, balance). This gives the house themes of partnership, diplomacy, and the quest for relational harmony.

Planets in the 7th House

Sun: Identity through partnership. Needs relationship to feel complete, can lose self in other.

Moon: Emotional security through partnership. Nurturing relationships, may be codependent.

Mercury: Communicative partnerships, intellectual connection. Needs mental rapport.

Venus: Natural placement—harmonious partnerships, attracts love easily. Values relationship highly.

Mars: Passionate partnerships, can be combative. Attracts assertive or aggressive partners.

Jupiter: Abundant partnerships, optimistic about relationships. May marry for growth or status.

Saturn: Serious partnerships, delayed marriage. Committed but may feel burdened by relationship.

Uranus: Unconventional partnerships, sudden relationships. Needs freedom in commitment.

Neptune: Idealized partnerships, spiritual connection. Can be delusional about partner.

Pluto: Intense partnerships, transformative relationships. Power dynamics, obsessive connections.

Jungian Interpretation

Psychological Theme: The 7th house represents projection—the qualities you disown in yourself and seek in partners. It's the shadow made visible through relationship, the mirror that shows you what you need to integrate.

Shadow: Codependency, losing self in relationship, projecting disowned qualities, people-pleasing

Integration: Owning projected qualities, balancing self with other, healthy interdependence

Shadow Work Questions

  • What qualities do I seek in partners that I deny in myself?
  • Am I losing myself to maintain the relationship?
  • Do I need a partner to feel complete?
  • Where am I avoiding conflict to keep peace?
  • How can I be in relationship while remaining whole?
