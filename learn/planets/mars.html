Mars

The Warrior • Action • Desire • Courage

Mars is the planet of action, desire, and courage. It governs how you assert yourself, pursue goals, express anger, and channel sexual energy. Mars is the warrior, the athlete, and the initiator. This is your drive, your will to act, and your capacity for courage.

Mythology & Symbolism

Mars (Ares in Greek) is the god of war, representing raw masculine energy, aggression, and the will to conquer. Unlike strategic Athena, Mars is impulsive, passionate, and direct. The red planet symbolizes blood, fire, and life force. Mars energy is about survival, competition, and the courage to fight for what you want.

Core Qualities

  • Rulership: Aries (traditional and modern), Scorpio (traditional only)
  • Exaltation: Capricorn (at 28°)
  • Detriment: Libra and Taurus
  • Fall: Cancer (at 28°)
  • Element: Fire (action, spirit)
  • Glyph: ♂ (shield and spear of the warrior)

Mars Represents

  • Action: How you initiate, pursue goals, and get things done
  • Desire: What you want and how you go after it
  • Anger: How you express frustration and aggression
  • Sexuality: Physical passion, sexual drive (especially masculine)
  • Courage: Your capacity for bravery and risk-taking
  • Competition: How you handle conflict and rivalry
  • Energy: Physical vitality, stamina, athleticism
  • Independence: Self-reliance and autonomy

Mars Through the Signs

♈ Aries: Pure, direct action. Fearless courage and drive.
♉ Taurus: Slow, steady action. Patient pursuit of goals.

♋ Cancer: Emotional action, protective courage. fights for family.
♌ Leo: Dramatic action, confident courage. Creative passion.

♎ Libra: Diplomatic action, fights for fairness.
♏ Scorpio: Intense, strategic action. Powerful will.
♐ Sagittarius: Adventurous action, optimistic energy.
♑ Capricorn: Disciplined, strategic action. Ambitious pursuit.
♒ Aquarius: Unconventional action, innovative courage.
♓ Pisces: Intuitive action, compassionate courage.

Mars Through the Houses

  • 1st House: Assertive identity, athletic body, courageous persona
  • 2nd House: Fights for resources, earns through action
  • 3rd House: Competitive communication, argumentative
  • 4th House: Fights for family, energetic home life
  • 5th House: Passionate creativity, competitive romance
  • 6th House: Energetic work, fights for health
  • 7th House: Attracts assertive partners, competitive relationships
  • 8th House: Intense sexuality, transformative action
  • 9th House: Adventurous philosophy, fights for beliefs
  • 10th House: Ambitious career, competitive public image
  • 11th House: Fights for causes, activist energy
  • 12th House: Hidden anger, spiritual warrior

Mars Retrograde

Mars retrogrades every 2 years for about 2-3 months. During this time:

  • Action: Delays, frustration, internalized energy
  • Anger: Passive-aggression, repressed rage surfacing
  • Sexuality: Decreased libido or sexual confusion
  • Opportunity: Review goals, redirect energy, heal anger

Natal Mars Retrograde: If you were born with Mars retrograde, you internalize anger and action. May struggle with assertion but have deep reserves of courage. Action is more strategic than impulsive.

Key Aspects

Mars-Sun

Powerful will, confident action. Leadership ability, athletic. Can be aggressive or egotistical.

Mars-Moon

Emotional action, protective courage. Quick emotional reactions. Can be volatile or defensive.

Mars-Venus

Passionate desire, artistic action. Attraction and pursuit aligned. Can be impulsive in love.

Mars-Jupiter

Expansive action, optimistic courage. Adventurous, generous. Can overextend or be reckless.

Mars-Saturn

Disciplined action, controlled anger. Strategic courage, patient pursuit. Can be frustrated or blocked.

Mars-Uranus

Sudden action, rebellious courage. Innovative, unpredictable. Can be accident-prone or explosive.

Mars-Neptune

Intuitive action, compassionate courage. Artistic passion. Can be confused, passive, or escapist.

Mars-Pluto

Intense action, transformative courage. Powerful will, obsessive pursuit. Can be ruthless or destructive.

Jungian Interpretation

Archetype: The Warrior, The Hero, The Animus (masculine principle)

Function: Mars represents the ego's will to act, assert, and survive. It's the capacity for courage and the drive to individuate.

Shadow: Aggression, violence, recklessness, repressed anger

Integration: Balancing assertion with receptivity, courage with compassion

Shadow Work for Mars

Questions to explore:

  • How do I express anger? Is it healthy or destructive?
  • Where do I assert myself too aggressively or not enough?
  • What am I truly fighting for?
  • How can I channel my energy constructively?
  • Where does my courage become recklessness?
