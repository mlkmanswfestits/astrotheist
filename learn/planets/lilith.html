Lilith

The Dark Moon • Wild Feminine • Sovereignty • Rage

Lilith (Black Moon Lilith) represents your wild, untamed feminine power. It shows where you refuse to submit, where you've been shamed or exiled, and where you reclaim your sovereignty. Lilith is the dark goddess, the first feminist, and the shadow of the feminine. This is your capacity for raw authenticity, sexual power, and righteous rage—but also where you may feel cast out or demonized.

Mythology & Symbolism

Lilith was Adam's first wife in Jewish mythology, created as his equal from the same earth. When she refused to submit to him sexually (specifically, to lie beneath him), she was cast out of Eden and demonized. She represents the feminine that refuses patriarchal control—wild, sexual, autonomous, and unapologetic. Lilith chose exile over submission, becoming the archetype of female sovereignty and rage against oppression.

Core Qualities

  • Astronomical Type: Mathematical point (lunar apogee—Moon's farthest point from Earth)
  • Orbital Period: ~9 years (~9 months per sign)
  • Types: Black Moon Lilith (most common), Asteroid Lilith, Dark Moon Lilith
  • Rulership: Some say Scorpio (debated)
  • Glyph: ⚸ (Black Moon crescent)

Lilith Represents

  • Wild Feminine: Untamed, raw, authentic feminine power
  • Sovereignty: Refusing to submit, claiming autonomy
  • Sexual Power: Uninhibited sexuality, erotic freedom
  • Rage: Righteous anger at oppression and injustice
  • Exile: Where you've been cast out, shamed, or demonized
  • Taboo: What's forbidden, repressed, or considered "too much"
  • Shadow Feminine: The dark goddess, the witch, the femme fatale
  • Reclamation: Taking back your power, owning your darkness

Lilith & Sexuality

Lilith is deeply connected to female sexuality—specifically, sexuality that is autonomous, not for male pleasure or procreation. She represents:

  • Sexual sovereignty and choice
  • Pleasure for its own sake
  • Refusing sexual submission or objectification
  • Reclaiming sexuality after shame or trauma
  • The "dangerous" or "too much" sexual woman

Lilith & Rage

Lilith holds righteous rage—anger at patriarchy, oppression, and being silenced. This rage is not destructive but transformative when channeled:

  • Setting fierce boundaries
  • Speaking truth to power
  • Refusing to be diminished
  • Protecting the vulnerable
  • Dismantling unjust systems

Jungian Interpretation

Archetype: The Dark Goddess, The Wild Woman, The Witch

Function: Lilith represents the shadow of the feminine—the parts of female power that patriarchy has demonized and women have repressed.

Shadow: Destructive rage, sexual manipulation, victimhood, self-exile

Integration: Reclaiming exiled parts, owning your power, transforming rage into sovereignty

Working with Lilith

Key Principles:

  • Lilith shows where you've been shamed—reclaim it
  • Your "too much" is your power
  • Rage is information—listen to it
  • Sovereignty means choosing yourself
  • The dark feminine is not evil—it's whole

Shadow Work for Lilith

Questions to explore:

  • Where have I been shamed for my power?
  • What parts of myself have I exiled to be accepted?
  • How can I channel my rage constructively?
  • Where do I submit when I should claim sovereignty?
  • What would it mean to be unapologetically myself?
