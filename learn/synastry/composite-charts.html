Composite Charts

The Composite Chart is not you. It is not them. It is **The Relationship** as a third, independent living entity.

1. The Mechanics: Midpoints vs. Davison

There are two primary ways to calculate a relationship chart, and both offer unique insights:

  • The Midpoint Method (Standard Composite): This is a mathematical abstraction. It takes the mathematical midpoint between your Sun and theirs, your Moon and theirs, and so on. It is not a "real" chart of a moment in time, but a symbolic map of the relationship's inner core. It is the most common method used by professional astrologers today.
  • The Davison Chart: This method finds the exact midpoint in time and space between the two births. It creates a "real" birth chart for the relationship, which can then be read like a natal chart, complete with its own transits and progressions. Many find the Davison more reliable for predictive timing.

2. Planetary Placements in the Composite

The planets in the composite chart tell us the "how" and "what" of the relationship:

  • Composite Sun: The fuel and identity of the union. A 1st house Sun makes the relationship highly visible; a 12th house Sun may mean the relationship is kept secret or is highly private.
  • Composite Moon: The emotional rhythm and "nesting" habits. If the composite Moon is in a hard aspect to Saturn, the relationship may feel emotionally dry or heavy, regardless of how much the individuals love each other.
  • Composite Mercury: The dialogue. A strong Mercury in the composite is often the reason couples can talk through any crisis.
  • Composite Venus: What the relationship values and how it expresses affection. In the 2nd house, the relationship may be focused on physical comfort and wealth.

  • 1st/5th/7th/8th Houses: These are the "Relationship Houses." If the majority of planets are here, the couple is focused on the connection itself. It feels romantic, erotic, and partnered.
  • 2nd/6th/10th Houses: These are the "Work Houses." If the composite is emphasized here, the couple may feel more like business partners or a unit that gets a lot of practical things done. This is excellent for stability but can sometimes feel "all work and no play."
  • Angular Houses (1, 4, 7, 10): These are the most powerful. Any planet on a composite angle becomes a defining characteristic of the union that the world will notice.

The Final Synthesis

A relationship with "bad synastry" but a "good composite" can be a very successful, long-term business or marriage where the two people build something great even if they rub each other the wrong way personally. Conversely, great synastry with a chaotic composite can feel like a "lightning strike" romance that has nowhere to go.
