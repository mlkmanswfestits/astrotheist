Synastry Basics: Love Compatibility

Decoding the Chemistry Between Two Charts

January 18, 2026 • 7 min read

Most dating advice boils down to: "Are you a Leo? He's a Libra? Great match!" But synastry—the art of comparing two natal charts—goes infinitely deeper than just Sun signs.

Synastry is not just about checking boxes. It's about understanding the specific energy exchange between two souls. It reveals where you flow easily, where you trigger each other, and what the karmic purpose of the relationship is.

The Big Three of Synastry

1. Moon-Moon Connections

This is the bedrock of domestic compatibility. If your Moons are in compatible elements (e.g., Water and Earth), you instinctively understand each other's emotional needs. Harmonious Moon aspects suggest you can live together peacefully.

2. Venus-Mars Chemistry

This is the spark. Venus represents what you love and value, while Mars represents how you pursue it (and sexual drive). A Venus-Mars conjunction or trine creates instant, magnetic physical attraction.

3. Saturn Glue

This is the commitment. For a relationship to last long-term, you need Saturn contacts. While they can feel heavy or restrictive, Saturn binds people together through duty and time.

4. Mercury Rappor

This is communication. Good Mercury contacts ensure you can resolve conflicts. Hard Mercury aspects suggest misunderstandings will be a recurring theme.

Red Flags to Watch For

While no aspect is inherently "bad," some require much more work:

  • Mars square Pluto: Can indicate power struggles, volatility, or domination issues.
  • Saturn square Moon: One person (Saturn) may feel cold or critical to the other (Moon), leading to emotional starvation.
  • Neptune opposite Venus: The relationship may be based on a fantasy or illusion that eventually dissolves.

The House Overlays

It's also crucial to see where someone's planets fall in your houses. If their Sun falls in your 7th House, they embody your ideal partner. If their planets pack your 8th House, the relationship will be intense and transformational.
